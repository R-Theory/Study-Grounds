## **CATEGORY: SCHOOLS OF THOUGHT - HIGH YIELD**

|FRONT (Question/Term)|BACK (Answer/Definition)|
|---|---|
|**Structuralism** - Main question?|**"WHAT elements is consciousness made of?"**|
|**Structuralism** - Leader?|**Edward Titchener**|
|**Structuralism** - Method?|**Introspection** (trained self-observation of one's own mental experiences)|
|**Structuralism** - Inspiration/analogy?|**Periodic table** - break consciousness into basic elements like chemistry|
|What is **introspection**?|A technique where trained observers describe their own conscious experiences in detail|
|What's the problem with introspection?|Too **subjective** - different people report different things; can't verify accuracy; can't study unconscious; can't use with children/animals|
|**Functionalism** - Main question?|**"WHY does consciousness exist? What PURPOSE does it serve?"**|
|**Functionalism** - Leaders?|**William James**, Stanley Hall|
|**Functionalism** - What inspired it?|**Darwin's theory of evolution** - if consciousness exists, it must help us survive/adapt|
|**Structuralism vs. Functionalism** - Key difference?|**Structuralism** = WHAT (parts/elements)<br>**Functionalism** = WHY (purpose/adaptation)|
|**Psychoanalysis** - Main idea?|**Unconscious mind** drives behavior; we cope with sexual urges|
|**Psychoanalysis** - Leader?|**Sigmund Freud**|
|**Psychoanalysis** - Methods?|Dream analysis, free association, analyzing slips of tongue|
|**Behaviorism** - Main idea?|Only study **OBSERVABLE BEHAVIOR**; ignore thoughts/consciousness; environment shapes everything|
|**Behaviorism** - Leaders?|**John B. Watson** (1900s-1920s)<br>**B.F. Skinner** (1950s onward)|
|**Behaviorism** - Methods?|Experiments with **rewards and punishments** (conditioning)|
|What did behaviorism's rise facilitate in research?|Increased use of **animals** for research (since you don't need to ask about thoughts)|
|Quote Watson's "dozen infants" statement (start of it)|"Give me a dozen healthy infants, well-formed, and my own special world to bring them up in and I'll guarantee to take any one at random and train him to become any type of specialist I might select..."|
|What does Watson's quote demonstrate?|Extreme **environmental determinism** - belief that environment (not genes) completely shapes behavior|
|What did **B.F. Skinner** say about behavior?|"Organisms tend to repeat responses that lead to positive outcomes, and they tend not to repeat responses that lead to neutral or negative outcomes"|
|What did Skinner say about free will?|Free will is an **illusion**|
|**Humanism** - Main idea?|Emphasize human **potential, free will, and personal growth**|
|**Humanism** - Leaders?|**Carl Rogers** and **Abraham Maslow**|
|**Humanism** - When did it emerge?|1950s (as reaction against psychoanalysis and behaviorism)|

## **CATEGORY: KEY TERMS & CONCEPTS**

|FRONT (Question/Term)|BACK (Answer/Definition)|
|---|---|
|**Consciousness**|Awareness of internal and external stimuli (what Wundt and early psychologists studied)|
|**Adaptation**|Inherited characteristic that gives survival or reproductive advantage (key to functionalism)|
|**Stream of consciousness**|William James's idea that consciousness flows continuously like a river, not static elements|
|**Unconscious**|Mental processes outside awareness that influence behavior (Freud's focus)|
|**Observable behavior**|Actions that can be seen and measured (behaviorism's sole focus)|
|**Free will**|Ability to make choices freely; humanists emphasized it, Skinner called it an illusion|
|**Personal growth**|Human potential for self-improvement and self-actualization (humanism's focus)|

## **CATEGORY: COMMON EXAM TRAPS**

|FRONT (Trap/Confusion)|BACK (Clarification)|
|---|---|
|Did James or Hall found the first US lab?|**Hall** founded first **formal** lab (1882); James had earlier **informal** lab (~1875) - exam will likely say Hall|
|Did James or Hall found American Journal of Psychology?|**Hall** (not James)|

|Is psychoanalysis the same as psychodynamic?|Psychoanalysis is Freud's specific theory; psychodynamic is broader approach based on his ideas|
|Who said free will is an illusion?|**B.F. Skinner** (behaviorist) - NOT Freud|
