# Chapter 3: Biological Bases of Behavior Flashcards

## **CATEGORY: BRAIN CELLS - NEURONS**

|FRONT (Question/Term)|BACK (Answer/Definition)|
|---|---|
|What is the function of neurons?|**Receives, processes, and transmits** information|
|What do **Dendrites** do?|**RECEIVE** information from other neurons|
|What does the **Soma (cell body)** do?|Contains nucleus and organelles; **PROCESSES** information|
|What does the **Axon** do?|**TRANSMITS** information away from cell body|
|What does the **Myelin sheath** do?|Insulation that **SPEEDS UP** signal transmission|
|What are **Nodes of Ranvier**?|Gaps in myelin where action potential "jumps"|
|What are **Terminal buttons**?|End of axon; **releases neurotransmitters**|
|What is **Saltatory conduction**?|Action potential "jumping" between nodes of Ranvier (faster due to myelin)|

## **CATEGORY: SYNAPSES & NEURAL SIGNALS**

|FRONT (Question/Term)|BACK (Answer/Definition)|
|---|---|
|What is **Axo-dendritic** synapse?|Axon to dendrite connection|
|What is **Axo-somatic** synapse?|Axon to soma (cell body) connection|
|What is **Axo-axonic** synapse?|Axon to axon connection|
|What is an **Action Potential**?|Electrical AND chemical signal that travels down the AXON|
|What does **All-or-none** mean?|Either fires completely or not at all (no partial firing)|
|What is the **Threshold of excitation**?|Minimum stimulation needed to trigger action potential|
|What is **Absolute refractory period**?|Brief time when neuron CANNOT fire again no matter what|
|What is **Relative refractory period**?|Time when neuron CAN fire but needs STRONGER stimulation|
|What is an **EPSP**?|Excitatory Postsynaptic Potential - makes neuron MORE likely to fire (depolarization)|
|What is an **IPSP**?|Inhibitory Postsynaptic Potential - makes neuron LESS likely to fire (hyperpolarization)|
|How are EPSPs and IPSPs different from action potentials?|EPSPs/IPSPs: No threshold, strength varies, degrades over distance (cable properties)|

## **CATEGORY: SUMMATION & BASAL RATE**

|FRONT (Question/Term)|BACK (Answer/Definition)|
|---|---|
|What is **Summation**?|EPSPs and IPSPs are added together|
|When does a postsynaptic neuron fire?|When EPSPs > IPSPs AND reaches threshold|
|When does a postsynaptic neuron NOT fire?|When IPSPs > EPSPs OR doesn't reach threshold|
|What is **Basal rate**?|Neurons are NOT silent at rest - they fire at a baseline rate|
|What happens with more EPSPs?|**Increased** firing rate|
|What happens with more IPSPs?|**Decreased** firing rate|

## **CATEGORY: NEUROTRANSMISSION PROCESS**

|FRONT (Question/Term)|BACK (Answer/Definition)|
|---|---|
|Step 1 of neurotransmission?|Action potential reaches **terminal buttons**|
|Step 2 of neurotransmission?|**Exocytosis** - neurotransmitter released from vesicles|
|Step 3 of neurotransmission?|Neurotransmitter diffuses across **synapse** (fluid-filled gap)|
|What is the **presynaptic neuron**?|Neuron that SENDS the message|
|What is the **postsynaptic neuron**?|Neuron that RECEIVES the message|
|What happens after neurotransmitter crosses synapse?|Binds to matching **receptor** at **binding site**|
|What does binding cause?|EPSP or IPSP in postsynaptic neuron|
|What happens after neurotransmitter unbinds?|**Reuptake** (recycled) and/or **enzymatic degradation** (broken down)|

## **CATEGORY: HERITABILITY STUDY METHODS - EXPANDED**

|FRONT (Question/Term)|BACK (Answer/Definition)|
|---|---|
|What is a **Family Study**?|Compares trait similarity among family members with **different degrees of genetic relatedness** (parents, siblings, cousins)|
|What is an **Adoption Study**?|Compares adopted children to their **biological parents** (genes) vs. **adoptive parents** (environment)|
|Why are adoption studies BETTER than family studies for estimating heritability?|Family members share BOTH genes AND environment; adoption studies **separate** genetic from environmental influences|
|Advantage of family studies?|Easy to conduct; can include many relatives|
|Disadvantage of family studies?|Cannot separate genetic from environmental effects (families share both)|
|Advantage of adoption studies?|Cleanly separates nature vs. nurture|
|Disadvantage of adoption studies?|Harder to find participants; adoptive families may not be representative; selective placement (agencies match families)|
|If an adopted child is MORE similar to biological parents than adoptive parents, what does this suggest?|**Genetic influence** is stronger for that trait|
|If an adopted child is MORE similar to adoptive parents than biological parents, what does this suggest?|**Environmental influence** is stronger for that trait|
